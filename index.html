About me

I'm Archit Bahirat, a final-year undergraduate student pursuing a B.Tech in Materials Engineering at the esteemed IIT Gandhinagar. Since 2022, I've had the privilege of working in Prof. Saumyakanti Khatua's Nanoplasmonics research lab, where I've gained invaluable hands-on experience with cutting-edge laboratory equipment and characterization techniques. My primary research focus has been on developing plasmonic nanoparticles for green energy applications. I'm incredibly passionate about the development of novel materials that can drive sustainable and clean energy technologies forward, and I'm actively seeking collaborations and opportunities in the fields of materials engineering and green energy.

My research interests are diverse, covering areas such as plasmonics, 2-D materials, and catalysis. Plasmonics, with its unique optical properties, has always fascinated me, and I'm particularly excited about its potential applications in solar energy harvesting, sensing, and catalysis. Additionally, the world of 2-D materials, including graphene and metal diborides, holds immense promise for innovations in electronics, energy storage and ballistic purposes. I'm eager to delve deeper into these materials' properties and applications.

Internships

Summer Research Intern, University of Miami, Project Topic: Exploring the Photo-Thermal Effect: Investigation of Surface Temperature Changes in Gold Nanoprisms (AuTNPs) Exposed to different Laser Intensities

Under the guidance of Professor Giacomo Po, my research project explored the photo-thermal effect by investigating surface temperature changes in Gold Nanoprisms (AuTNPs) exposed to different laser intensities. I conducted Finite Element Method (FEM) simulations using COMSOL software, focusing on laser intensities of 88 mW/cm^2 and 100 mW/cm^2. Employing a plasmonic heating model, I accurately predicted and quantified surface temperature changes in AuTNPs, enhancing our understanding of their photothermal response. Additionally, I extended my research to include gold nanocubes, conducting simulations and calculations to deepen insights into the relationship between nanoparticle morphology and photo-thermal behavior.

Multiscale Modeling and Experimental Validation of Photo-Thermal Effects in Plasmonic Photocatalysis Using Gold Triangular Nanoprisms

Utilized the RF and Heat Transfer modules within COMSOL Multiphysics to simulate the intricate interplay between electromagnetic radiation, heat generation and heat transfer process. Investigation of Changes in Mechanical Properties of Polyurethane with TiB2 Nanosheets as Filler

I have been actively involved in the synthesis of Titanium boride nanosheets, starting from bulk TiB2, by employing high-energy ball-milling techniques. This process has enabled the transformation of the bulk material into nanoscale sheets, which is a significant advancement in materials science. Presently, I am engaged in infusing different percentages of these nanosheets into polyurethane to design novel composites with enhanced mechanical properties. To comprehensively analyze these synthesized nanocomposites, I have employed various techniques such as the Universal Testing Machine (UTM) for mechanical testing, Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) to study thermal behavior, and Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA) for thermal stability assessments. This multifaceted approach allows for a thorough understanding of how the incorporation of Titanium boride nanosheets impacts the performance of polyurethane composites, and it holds great potential for a wide range of applications in the field of advanced materials.

Tuning photocatalytic activity of Plasmonic nanoparticles through surface ligands

I have been actively engaged in the synthesis of AuTNPs, demonstrating proficiency in nanoparticle fabrication techniques. During this research, I attached various aromatic thiol ligands to the surface of AuTNPs and confirmed their presence by conducting Surface Enhanced Raman Spectroscopy (SERS), enabling a thorough investigation of their influence on photocatalytic performance. In addition, I meticulously designed and implemented dye degradation reactions, employing both 532nm and 808nm lasers for our photocatalytic studies. These efforts have contributed significantly to our understanding of how ligands can impact the photocatalytic properties of AuTNPs and have the potential to advance the field of nanomaterial-based catalysis.

Effective utilization of NIR-II photons via plasmothermal Si-H activation

I have gained valuable hands-on experience in the synthesis of gold triangular nanoprisms (AuTNPs) and gold nanospheres (AuNS) for use as highly efficient photocatalysts. Through rigorous experimentation, I have fine-tuned the photocatalysis parameters, which has enabled the optimization of reaction kinetics and led to improvements in the selectivity and reactivity of the process. This innovative approach not only holds promise for sustainable waste management but also stands out for its novelty and excellent photon-to-hydrogen conversion efficiency, resulting in the publication of this significant research in a reputable scientific journal.

Deposition of AlN films on SLG substrate

I focused on the deposition of Aluminum Nitride (AlN) films with varying nitride concentration onto glass substrates using the RF-magnetron sputtering technique.. Firstly, I utilized Grazing Incidence X-ray Diffraction (GIXRD) to investigate the structural properties of the films. GIXRD allowed me to analyze the crystallographic structure, orientation, and phase composition of the AlN films, providing valuable insights into their crystalline nature. In addition to structural characterization, I conducted Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) imaging to examine the surface morphology and microstructure of the AlN films. SEM provided high-resolution images, enabling a detailed assessment of the film's surface topography and any potential defects or irregularities. Furthermore, I employed UV-visible spectroscopy (UV-vis) to study the optical properties of the AlN films. UV-vis spectroscopy allowed me to explore how the varying nitride concentration influenced the film's optical absorption and transparency characteristics, which are crucial considerations for various applications

Synthesis and Recrystallization of Ni-20Co Alloys

I conducted a comprehensive study involving the synthesis of Ni-Co-20 alloy through the electric arc melting technique. Following the initial alloy synthesis, I further processed the samples by subjecting them to cold rolling, with variations of 50% and 80% reduction in thickness, to investigate the impact of cold working on the alloy's properties. Subsequently, annealing experiments were performed on these samples at different temperatures, each for approximately two hours, in order to study the microstructural and phase transformations that occurred during the annealing process. In my analysis, X-ray Diffraction (XRD) identified crystalline phases and phase changes, Energy-Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy (EDS) determined alloy composition, and Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) visualized microstructures and defects, providing a comprehensive understanding of the alloy's properties.
